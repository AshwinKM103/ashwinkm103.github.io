We study whether a human-inspired **learn–master–teach** lifecycle can improve the
mathematical reasoning of large language models beyond static fine-tuning. The pipeline
combines curriculum-grounded learning, interactive concept refinement, feedback-driven
practice, multi-teacher distillation, self-reflection, hallucination mitigation, and
pedagogical reinforcement learning. On CBSE-style math benchmarks the approach yields
**+10–17 percentage-point** accuracy gains over the base model.
